02The 5W1H × BaZi Architecture

Six questions.
Each with an energetic anchor.

The West asks. The East answers. Five questions and one method — each mapped to a BaZi anchor that makes your development plan specific to your chart, not generic.

Origins起源 · qǐ yuán

The six questions trace back to Aristotle, who in his Nicomachean Ethics identified seven "circumstances of inquiry" — who, what, when, where, why, how, and by what means — as the elements every careful examination must consider before judging an action. Medieval scholastics adopted the list under the Latin mnemonic quis, quid, ubi, quibus auxiliis, cur, quomodo, quando, and used it to interrogate cases of conscience, criminal acts, and rhetorical claims. The framework outlived its theological origins and became the modern investigator's checklist — what journalists, courts, and consultants still use to make sense of any situation.

It was Rudyard Kipling who canonized the modern compressed form. In his 1902 poem from Just So Stories, he wrote: "I keep six honest serving-men. They taught me all I knew. Their names are What and Why and When and How and Where and Who." The 5W1H acronym took root from there, becoming the universal grammar of inquiry across journalism, project management, problem-solving, and now leadership development.

The DH Framework's contribution is the synthesis. The West asks; the East answers. 5W1H supplies the questions every development plan must answer — but the answers, generic to most frameworks, are differentiated by your BaZi chart. Two professionals reading the same self-help book end up with the same prescription. Two professionals running this framework — even with the same job and aspiration — end up with completely different IDPs because their charts are different. The questions are universal; the answers, finally, are yours.

A synthesis of Aristotle, Kipling, and Classical BaZi · 五行十神 · wǔ xíng shí shén

09Quick Glossary

Terms, decoded.

Key terms you will encounter as you go deeper.

BaZi Concepts
BaZi
"Eight Characters." Chinese metaphysical chart based on year, month, day, hour of birth. The foundational input for this framework.
Day Master
The Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar. Represents you — your core element. All other elements in the chart are read relative to it.
Useful God
The element that brings your chart into balance. Your remedy and strategic advantage — what you need more of.
Luck Pillar
A 10-year energetic phase, expressed in stem-branch terms. Your "current decade" — the dominant environmental energy shaping your window.
Period 9
2024–2043. A Feng Shui era favoring Fire industries — AI, media, technology, and vision-based work. The macro context for all current IDPs.
Sheng Cycle
Generating cycle. Wood feeds Fire → Fire makes Earth → Earth produces Metal → Metal carries Water → Water nourishes Wood.
Ke Cycle
Controlling cycle. Wood breaks Earth → Earth dams Water → Water extinguishes Fire → Fire melts Metal → Metal cuts Wood.
Kong Wang
"Void." Energetic blanks in your chart. Areas of low resonance — often addressed via the Healer Driver and P-Island mode.
Framework Concepts
5 Strategic Drivers
The five reasons a person develops: Healer (rebalance), Ace (compound talent), Surfer (ride timing), Soldier (meet demand), Visionary (build legacy).
P-Island
Study mode — withdrawal for theoretical mastery. Certifications, deep reading, mental models. Triggered by Healer and Visionary Drivers.
Q-Ways
Action mode — learning through real-world collision. Projects, public performance, deadlines. Triggered by Soldier, Surfer, and Ace Drivers.
Life Stage
Crisis · Growth · Actualization · Expertise. Determines how the 5 Driver scores are weighted when generating your IDP.
10 Gods
Five functional groups (Officer, Wealth, Output, Resource, Companion), each with a Direct and Indirect expression. Maps capability to relational archetypes.
